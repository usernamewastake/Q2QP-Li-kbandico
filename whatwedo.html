What We Do

Initiatives

Climate change poses the greatest threat to the most vulnerable who are often the ones contributing the least to its escalation. The global climate crisis affects everything that CARE does and threatens our vision of a world of hope, tolerance and social justice where poverty has been overcome and people live in dignity and security. As the breadth and impact of the climate emergency are expected to worsen in the coming future, CARE must do all it can to ensure that our actions are part of a global, urgent, effective and equitable response.

Activities

    • Incorporating climate change across the spectrum from humanitarian assistance to long-term development.
      Generating evidence from practical experience on the ground to feed into program development, policy analysis and advocacy on climate change, both nationally and internationally.
      Working in partnerships with other organizations and networks.
